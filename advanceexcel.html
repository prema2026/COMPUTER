Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to know MS Excel basics before joining?
+
Basic familiarity with Excel (opening files, entering data, simple SUM formulas) is recommended. If you are completely new to Excel, we suggest first completing our MS Office course which covers Excel basics, or we can arrange a short bridge session at the start.
What jobs can I get after completing this course?
+
Advanced Excel is required for roles like MIS Executive, Data Analyst, Accounts Executive, Finance Assistant, Operations Analyst, HR Executive, and Back Office Executive. Many government and banking sector roles also require advanced Excel proficiency.
Is VBA / Macro programming included in this course?
+
Yes! Macros and VBA is a full module (20 hours) in this course. You will learn to record macros, write VBA code, create custom functions (UDFs), build UserForms, and automate report generation — all from scratch.
Which Excel version will be used for training?
+
We primarily teach on Microsoft Excel 2019 and Microsoft 365. Dynamic array functions (FILTER, XLOOKUP etc.) are taught separately as Excel 365 features and clearly marked. All core concepts apply to Excel 2016, 2019, and 365.
How is Advance Excel different from the MS Office Excel module?
+
The MS Office course covers Excel basics — data entry, simple formulas, basic charts, and formatting. The Advance Excel course goes much deeper — complex formulas (VLOOKUP, SUMIFS, array formulas), Pivot Tables with Slicers, Dashboard building, Power Query, Macros, VBA, and real business projects. It is designed to make you an Excel power user.
Are there morning, evening, or weekend batches?
+
Yes, multiple batch timings are available including morning, afternoon, and evening batches on weekdays. Weekend batches may be available based on demand. Please call us at 0755-2687474 to check current schedules and available seats.
